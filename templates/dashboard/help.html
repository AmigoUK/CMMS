How CMMS Works

{# ── Overview ──────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Overview

CMMS (Computerised Maintenance Management System) tracks maintenance work from the moment a problem is spotted through to resolution. Every piece of work follows a clear path:

Report Problem Triage Work Order Complete Asset History

Everything you enter is preserved — photos, notes, parts used, time spent — building a maintenance history for each asset.

{# ── Multi-Site ────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Sites

The system manages multiple sites. Each site has its own locations, assets, requests, and work orders. If you have access to more than one site, use the site switcher in the top navigation bar to change your active site. All data you see is filtered to the currently selected site.

{# ── Roles ─────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
User Roles

What you can see and do depends on your role:

Role What you can do
User Report problems, upload photos, track your own requests. This is the role for anyone who spots an issue — operators, office staff, shop workers.
Contractor View and execute work orders assigned to you. Log time and notes. You only see jobs assigned to you — not the full queue or asset details.
Technician Everything a Contractor can do, plus: view all work orders for the site, view asset details and history, record parts used.
Supervisor Everything a Technician can do, plus: review and triage incoming requests, convert requests into work orders, assign work to technicians and contractors, manage assets, locations, and parts inventory, close work orders.
Admin Full access. Manage users, teams, and sites. Everything a Supervisor can do across all assigned sites.
{# ── Request Lifecycle ─────────────────────────────────────── #}
Requests — Reporting a Problem

Anyone with an account can submit a maintenance request. Keep it simple — describe what's wrong, where it is, and attach a photo if you can. A supervisor will review it.

Request statuses
New Just submitted. Waiting for a supervisor to review.
Acknowledged A supervisor has seen the request and confirmed it's valid.
In Progress A work order has been created from this request. Maintenance is underway.
Resolved The linked work order has been completed and the issue is fixed.
Closed Fully closed — no further action needed.
Cancelled Request was cancelled (duplicate, not valid, etc.).
{# ── Work Order Lifecycle ──────────────────────────────────── #}
Work Orders — Getting Work Done

A work order is the controlled maintenance task. It can be created from a request (most common) or directly by a supervisor for planned work like inspections or preventive maintenance.

Work order lifecycle
Open Assigned In Progress Completed Closed
Open Created but not yet assigned to anyone.
Assigned A technician or contractor has been assigned. Work hasn't started yet.
In Progress Work has started. The assignee is on the job.
On Hold Work paused — typically waiting for parts, access, or further information.
Completed Work is done. The technician has recorded what they found and what they did. Waiting for supervisor to review and close.
Closed Supervisor has reviewed and closed the work order. This is the final state.
Cancelled Work order was cancelled before completion.
What the technician records during execution
  • Checklist tasks — step-by-step items to tick off as work progresses.
  • Parts used — which parts were consumed and how many. Stock is automatically deducted.
  • Time spent — how long the work took (in minutes).
  • Photos and files — evidence of the work done or issues found.
  • Findings — what was discovered during the job (causes, observations, recommendations).
  • Completion notes — summary of the actions taken.
{# ── Work Order Types ──────────────────────────────────────── #}
Work Order Types
Corrective Fix something that is broken or not working properly. Most common type — usually created from a request.
Preventive Scheduled maintenance to prevent breakdowns — servicing, lubrication, filter changes, calibration.
Inspection Checking equipment condition without necessarily doing repair work — safety inspections, condition assessments, compliance checks.
Emergency Urgent unplanned work that cannot wait — safety hazards, critical equipment failure, production stoppage.
{# ── Priority ──────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Priority Levels
Low Cosmetic issues, minor inconveniences, nice-to-fix items. No impact on operations.
Medium Should be addressed within a few days. Operations can continue but the issue is noticeable.
High Significant impact. Workaround may exist but the problem needs prompt attention.
Critical Operations are stopped or a safety risk exists. Needs immediate action.
{# ── Assets ────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Assets

An asset is any piece of equipment, machine, or system that requires maintenance — ovens, mixers, fridges, electrical panels, HVAC units, etc. Each asset has:

  • Asset tag — a unique identifier (e.g., BM-OV-001)
  • Location — where it is installed
  • Status — operational, needs repair, out of service, or decommissioned
  • Criticality — how important it is to operations (low to critical)
  • Maintenance history — all past work orders linked to this asset

When you report a problem, linking it to the correct asset helps build a history. If the same mixer keeps breaking down, the history makes that pattern visible.

{# ── Locations ─────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Locations

Locations are organised in a hierarchy: Building > Floor > Area > Room. When reporting a problem, you can pick a location even if you don't know the specific asset. The maintenance team will identify the right equipment.

{# ── Parts ─────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Parts Inventory

The parts inventory tracks spare parts and consumables. When a technician records parts used on a work order, the stock is automatically deducted. Parts flagged as low stock (quantity at or below the minimum) are highlighted so supervisors can reorder before running out.

{# ── Teams ─────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Teams & Contractors

Technicians and contractors are organised into teams. Teams are either internal (your own maintenance staff) or external (contractor companies). Contractors can only see and work on jobs specifically assigned to them — they do not have access to the full queue, asset records, or parts costs.

A supervisor assigns work to the right person based on skills and availability. External contractors are typically assigned specialist work (refrigeration, electrical, etc.).

{# ── Quick guide per role ──────────────────────────────────── #}
Quick Guide — What Do I Do?

  1. Click Report a Problem on the dashboard (or go to Requests > New Request).
  2. Give it a short title (e.g., "Fridge not cold").
  3. Describe what's wrong — be specific about what you see/hear/smell.
  4. Pick the location and asset if you know them (optional).
  5. Set the urgency — when in doubt, use Medium.
  6. Attach a photo if it helps show the problem.
  7. Submit. You'll get a request number and can track the status from My Requests.

  1. Check My Assigned Work Orders on the dashboard.
  2. Open the work order to see what needs doing.
  3. Click Start Work when you begin.
  4. Work through the checklist, ticking items as you go.
  5. Record any parts you used.
  6. Log your time (how many minutes the job took).
  7. Upload photos of the work done or issues found.
  8. When finished, click Complete — describe what you found and what you did.

  1. Check the Triage Queue on the dashboard for new requests.
  2. Open each new request — review the description and photos.
  3. Click Acknowledge to confirm the request is valid.
  4. Click Convert to Work Order to create a job from the request.
  5. Edit the work order to add a checklist, set the type and priority, pick a due date.
  6. Assign the work order to a technician or contractor.
  7. Monitor progress — check for overdue or on-hold work orders.
  8. When work is completed, review the findings and Close the work order.

As admin, you have full supervisor access plus system management:

  • Admin > Users — create accounts, assign roles, assign users to sites, reset passwords.
  • Admin > Teams — create internal teams or external contractor companies.
  • Admin > Sites — add or edit site locations managed by the system.
  • Locations — set up the building/floor/area hierarchy for each site.
  • Assets — register equipment with tags, manufacturers, and criticality ratings.
  • Parts — set up the spare parts inventory with minimum stock levels.
