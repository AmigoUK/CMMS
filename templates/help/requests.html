Understanding Requests

A request is a report of a problem. Once submitted, it moves through a lifecycle as the maintenance team reviews and resolves it.

{# ── Request Lifecycle ──────────────────────────────────────── #}
Request Lifecycle
{# Visual flow #}
New Acknowledged In Progress Resolved Closed
Status Meaning
New Just submitted, waiting for review
Acknowledged Supervisor has confirmed it's valid
In Progress A work order has been created
Resolved Work is done, issue fixed
Closed Fully closed, no further action
Cancelled Request was cancelled
{# ── Tracking Your Requests ─────────────────────────────────── #}
Tracking Your Requests

After submitting, you can track your request from the Dashboard or the Requests page.

Click on any request to see its full details, activity timeline, and linked work order.

{# ── Adding Comments ────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Adding Comments

You can add comments to your request at any time (unless it's closed or cancelled).

Use comments to provide updates, clarify details, or ask questions.

Supervisors can also comment — check back for responses.

{# ── Selecting an Asset ─────────────────────────────────────── #}
Selecting an Asset

When submitting a request, the asset selector is searchable. Start typing at least 3 characters of the asset name or code and matching results will appear in a dropdown.

This makes it quick to find the right equipment even when there are hundreds of items in the register.

{# ── Priority Guidance ────────────────────────────────────── #}
Priority Guidance

Choosing the right priority helps the maintenance team respond appropriately:

  • Not Urgent — Cosmetic or minor issue. No safety concern, no impact on production. Example: chipped paint, squeaky door.
  • Normal — Should be fixed within a few days. Operations continue but the issue needs attention. Example: dripping tap, flickering light.
  • Urgent — Significant disruption or risk of escalation. Needs prompt action. Example: partial equipment failure, persistent leak.
  • Emergency — Safety hazard or complete equipment failure. Needs immediate response. Example: gas leak, fire door jammed, machine producing dangerous sparks.
{# ── Uploading Files ────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Uploading Files

You can upload photos and documents to your request.

Supported formats: JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF, PDF, Word, Excel.

Photos help technicians understand the problem before arriving on site.
