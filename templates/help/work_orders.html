Work Orders

A work order is the controlled maintenance task. It tracks what needs to be done, who's doing it, what parts and time were used, and what was found.

{# ── Work Order Lifecycle ───────────────────────────────────── #}
Work Order Lifecycle

Every work order moves through a series of statuses from creation to closure.

{# Flow diagram using badges #}
Open Assigned In Progress Completed Closed
Side branch from In Progress: On Hold Can be cancelled at any stage: Cancelled
Status Description
Open Work order has been created but not yet assigned to a technician.
Assigned A technician has been assigned and the work is awaiting start.
In Progress The technician has started working on the task.
On Hold Work is paused, typically waiting for parts, information, or access.
Completed The technician has finished the work and recorded their findings.
Closed A supervisor has reviewed and closed the work order. No further changes.
Cancelled The work order has been cancelled and will not be completed. Can be applied at any stage.
{# ── Executing a Work Order ─────────────────────────────────── #}
Executing a Work Order

Step-by-step guide for technicians carrying out maintenance work.

  1. Check your assignments — Go to the Dashboard and look at the "My Assigned Work Orders" section.
  2. Open the work order — Click on the work order to view its details, instructions, and any attached files.
  3. Start work — Click the Start Work button to change the status to In Progress.
  4. Work through the checklist — Complete each task in the checklist and tick items off as you go.
  5. Record parts used — Select each part from the dropdown, enter the quantity consumed. Stock is automatically deducted.
  6. Log time spent — Enter the number of minutes worked and add any notes about what was done.
  7. Upload photos — Take photos of the completed work and upload them as evidence.
  8. Complete the work order — Click Complete and describe what you found and what you did.
{# ── Work Order Types ───────────────────────────────────────── #}
Work Order Types

Work orders are categorised by the type of maintenance being performed.

Type Description
Corrective Reactive maintenance to fix something that has broken down or is malfunctioning.
Preventive Scheduled maintenance to prevent breakdowns, such as servicing, lubrication, or filter changes.
Inspection Routine checks and assessments to identify wear, damage, or potential issues before they cause failure.
Emergency Urgent, unplanned work required immediately due to safety risks or critical equipment failure.
{# ── PM-Generated Work Orders ──────────────────────────────── #}
PM-Generated Work Orders

The Preventive Maintenance (PM) Planner can automatically generate work orders based on scheduled tasks.

  • Automatic creation — When a PM task is due (based on its schedule and lead days), the system auto-generates a work order with the type set to "Preventive".
  • WO prefix — PM-generated work orders use a configurable prefix (set in Admin → PM Settings) so they are easy to identify, e.g. PM-20260408-001.
  • Pre-populated details — The generated work order inherits the PM task's description, checklist, assigned asset, and location.
  • Overdue tracking — PM tasks that pass their due date without a completed work order are flagged as overdue. Thresholds are configurable in PM Settings.
PM-generated work orders follow the same lifecycle as manually created ones. See the Admin Guide for PM Settings configuration.
{# ── Checklists ─────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Checklists

Checklists are task lists attached to a work order that guide technicians through each step of the job.

  • Each work order can have a checklist of individual tasks to complete.
  • Tick items off as you work through them — progress is saved automatically.
  • Supervisors can add checklist items when creating or editing a work order.
  • Checklists help ensure consistency and nothing is missed during maintenance.
{# ── Parts & Time ───────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Parts & Time

Recording parts and time against a work order keeps accurate maintenance records and manages inventory.

Parts Usage
  • Select a part from the dropdown on the work order detail page.
  • Enter the quantity used.
  • Stock levels are automatically deducted from inventory when parts are recorded.
  • If stock falls to or below the minimum level, a reorder alert is triggered.
Time Logging
  • Enter the number of minutes spent on the task.
  • Add a brief note describing what was done during that time.
  • Multiple time entries can be logged against a single work order.
  • Time logs help track labour costs and identify jobs that take longer than expected.
Stock validation: The system prevents adding parts when there is insufficient stock. If you attempt to use more than the available quantity, the entry will be rejected. Check stock levels or ask a supervisor to restock before trying again.
Part Usage Reversal

If parts were logged incorrectly, a Supervisor can reverse the usage entry from the work order detail page. Click the reverse icon next to the part entry. The stock quantity will be restored to inventory automatically.
