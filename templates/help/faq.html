Frequently Asked Questions

{# ── General ────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
General

CMMS stands for Computerised Maintenance Management System. It helps organisations track maintenance requests, manage work orders, monitor equipment, and keep on top of spare parts inventory — all in one place.

The quickest way is to scan the QR code label on the equipment. You can also log in and submit a request through the Requests page. See the Reporting a Problem guide for full details.

It depends on your organisation's settings. If an administrator has enabled anonymous reporting, anyone can submit a request by scanning a QR code — no account required. If anonymous reporting is disabled, you will need to sign in first.

Point your phone's camera at the QR code label on the equipment. Most modern phones will automatically recognise the code and show a link — tap it to open the reporting form in your browser. No special app is needed.
{# ── Requests ───────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Requests

  • Not Urgent — Minor issue, no immediate impact. Can wait for scheduled maintenance.
  • Normal — Standard priority. Should be addressed in the normal workflow.
  • Urgent — Significant impact on operations. Needs attention as soon as possible.
  • Emergency — Safety risk or critical failure. Requires immediate response.

If you are signed in, go to the Dashboard and check the "My Requests" section. You can also visit the Requests page to see all your submissions and their current status.

{# ── Work Orders ────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Work Orders

Your Dashboard shows a "My Assigned Work Orders" section listing all work orders currently assigned to you. This is the quickest way to see what needs your attention.

Click Start Work to begin, then work through the checklist, log any parts used and time spent, upload photos, and finally click Complete. You will be asked to describe what you found and what was done.

Yes. While a work order is In Progress, you can click the Put on Hold button. Use this when you are waiting for parts to arrive, need access to a locked area, or are waiting for further information before you can continue.

On the work order detail page, use the parts dropdown to select a part and enter the quantity used — stock is automatically deducted from inventory. For time, enter the number of minutes spent and add a note. You can add multiple entries for both parts and time.
{# ── Supervisors ────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Supervisors

Your Dashboard shows a triage queue with new and unacknowledged requests. Review each request and either acknowledge it (to mark it as seen and under review) or convert it directly into a work order if the issue is clear.

Open the request detail page and click the Convert to Work Order button. This creates a new work order pre-populated with the request details. You can then assign it to a technician and add a checklist.

Open the work order detail page and go to the Assignment section. Select a technician from the dropdown and save. The technician will see the work order in their "My Assigned Work Orders" on the Dashboard.

The reorder report shows all parts whose stock has fallen to or below their minimum level. Navigate to Parts → Reorder Report to view it. Items are grouped by supplier so you can place consolidated orders. The report is printable.
{# ── Admin ──────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Admin

Go to Admin → Users → New User. Fill in the user's name, email address, and set a temporary password. Assign them a role (Admin, Supervisor, Technician, or Reporter) and select which sites they should have access to.

Go to Admin → Settings and toggle the anonymous reporting option. When enabled, anyone who scans a QR code can submit a maintenance request without needing an account.

Go to the Property list page and click Print All QR Labels. Select the items you want labels for and click Print. You can also print a single label from any property detail page.

Click the icon in the navigation bar to toggle dark mode on or off. Your preference is remembered automatically, so the next time you open CMMS it will use your chosen theme.
