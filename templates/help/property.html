Property & Parts

Property covers all maintainable items — machines, tools, vehicles, and buildings. Parts are the spare parts and consumables used during maintenance.

{# ── Property Register ──────────────────────────────────────── #}
Property Register

The property register is a central record of every maintainable item in your organisation.

  • What is a property item? Any asset that requires maintenance — equipment, machinery, vehicles, tools, or building elements (doors, HVAC units, lifts, etc.).
  • Categories — Group items by type (e.g. Electrical, Mechanical, Plumbing) for easier filtering and reporting.
  • Tags — Apply free-form tags to items for flexible cross-category grouping.
  • Images — Upload photos to help technicians identify equipment quickly in the field.
Property Statuses
Status Meaning
Operational Item is working normally and available for use.
Needs Repair Item has a known issue and requires maintenance attention.
Out of Service Item is not operational and must not be used until repaired.
Decommissioned Item has been permanently retired and is no longer maintained.
{# ── QR Code Labels ─────────────────────────────────────────── #}
QR Code Labels

QR labels let anyone scan equipment to instantly report a problem or view its maintenance history.

Printing QR Labels
  1. Go to the Property list page.
  2. Click Print All QR Labels.
  3. Select the items you want to print labels for.
  4. Click Print — labels are formatted for standard label sheets.
Single Labels

You can also print a single QR label from any property detail page using the Print QR Label button.

{# ── Parts Inventory ────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Parts Inventory

Track spare parts and consumables so you always know what is in stock and when to reorder.

  • Stock levels — Each part has a current stock quantity, plus configurable minimum and maximum levels.
  • Minimum level — When stock falls to or below this threshold, a reorder alert is triggered.
  • Maximum level — The recommended upper limit to avoid overstocking.
  • Reorder alerts — Parts at or below their minimum level appear in the Reorder Report and are flagged on the dashboard.
  • Supplier info — Record the supplier name, contact details, and part number for easy reordering.
{# ── Part Compatibility ─────────────────────────────────────── #}
Part Compatibility

Link parts to the property items they are compatible with, so technicians always know which parts fit which equipment.

  • On a part detail page, you can link it to one or more property items — the part page then shows all compatible equipment.
  • On a property detail page, you can see all compatible parts — making it easy to find the right replacement during a repair.
  • Compatibility links work in both directions: linking a part to a property item also makes the part appear on that property item's page.
{# ── Reorder Report ─────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Reorder Report

The reorder report helps you stay on top of stock replenishment before you run out of critical parts.

  • What triggers it? Any part whose current stock is at or below its configured minimum level.
  • Where to find it: Navigate to Parts → Reorder Report.
  • Grouped by supplier — Items are grouped by their assigned supplier, making it easy to place consolidated orders.
  • Printable — The report is print-friendly so you can hand it to your purchasing team or take it with you.
