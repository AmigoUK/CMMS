Getting Started

This guide walks you through the basics of using CMMS, whether you're reporting a problem, executing maintenance work, or managing the system.

{# ── What is CMMS? ───────────────────────────────────────────── #}
What is CMMS?

CMMS (Computerised Maintenance Management System) is a system for tracking repairs, inspections, and preventive maintenance across one or more sites. Every piece of work follows a clear lifecycle:

Report Problem Triage Work Order Complete Asset History

Everything you enter is preserved -- photos, notes, parts used, time spent -- building a complete maintenance history for each asset.

{# ── Your Role ────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Your Role

What you can see and do depends on your role:

Role What you can do
User Report problems, upload photos, track your own requests.
Contractor View and execute work orders assigned to you. Log time, notes, and parts.
Technician Everything a Contractor can do, plus: view all work orders for the site, view asset details and history, record parts used.
Supervisor Everything a Technician can do, plus: triage incoming requests, convert to work orders, assign work, manage assets, locations, and parts inventory, close work orders.
Admin Full access. Everything a Supervisor can do, plus user, team, and site management.
{# ── First Steps by Role ─────────────────────────────────────── #}
First Steps by Role

Find your role below to see what to do first.

{# User / Shop Staff #}

  1. Go to the Dashboard.
  2. Click "Report a Problem".
  3. Fill in what's wrong -- describe the issue, pick a location, and attach a photo if you can.
  4. Track progress from "My Requests".
{# Contractor #}

  1. Check the Dashboard for work orders assigned to you.
  2. Open the work order to see what needs doing.
  3. Click Start Work when you begin.
  4. Log time, notes, and any parts used.
  5. Click Mark Complete when finished.
{# Technician #}

  1. Check the Dashboard for assigned work orders.
  2. Open the work order to see what needs doing.
  3. Click Start Work when you begin.
  4. Complete the checklist, log time and parts used.
  5. Click Mark Complete when finished.
{# Supervisor #}

  1. Check the Triage Queue for new requests.
  2. Acknowledge valid requests.
  3. Convert to Work Orders and add checklists, priority, and due dates.
  4. Assign work orders to technicians or contractors.
  5. Review and Close completed work orders.
{# Admin #}

  1. Set up Sites -- add the locations your organisation manages.
  2. Create Teams -- internal maintenance teams and external contractors.
  3. Add Users and assign roles and sites to each person.
  4. Configure Settings -- locations, property, parts inventory, and categories.
  5. Set up Suppliers and the Address Book for contact management.
  6. Configure the PM Planner to schedule preventive maintenance tasks automatically.
{# ── Dark Mode ───────────────────────────────────────────────── #}
Dark Mode

CMMS supports a dark colour scheme for comfortable use in low-light environments. Click the icon in the navigation bar to toggle between light and dark mode. Your preference is saved automatically.
